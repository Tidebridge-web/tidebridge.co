Gold Fell When the World Caught Fire.
Fifty Years of History Say That’s the Tell.

Missiles over the Gulf, oil surging, markets in retreat — and gold down a quarter from its record. The reflex is to call it a failed hedge. The record says something far more interesting.

In every major crisis of the past half-century — 1973, 1979, the Gulf War, September 11, 2020, 2022 — gold did the same thing first: it fell. Sold as the most liquid profit in a market scrambling for cash. And then, as the dust settled, it did the other thing.

Gold’s drawdown in each crisis (red), and where it traded twelve months after the low (blue). Approximate data; sources: LBMA, author calculations. Past performance is not indicative of future results.

The 2026 correction is already deeper than every one of those episodes but one. Sentiment, positioning and fund flows sit at decade extremes. And the two crises this one most resembles in structure produced the two largest recoveries in the entire dataset.

That is the pattern. The why — and whether it holds this time — is the subject of our new research note.
